GIS SuperPro Helps Save the Children in Los Angeles, California!!!

As a GIS Superhero Intern, I tackled the complex issue of methane-producing landfills and their potential impact on the health of children and young adults in Los Angeles. Armed with GIS skills and a passion for environmental justice, I dove into the project assigned by my internship coordinator, Leonela. I could comprehensively answer the questions posed by analyzing multiple GIS layers, including data on schools, supervisorial districts, methane-producing landfills, and income per capita in census tracts. I then identified the supervisorial districts of census tracts in the bottom 50 percent of income per capita, containing schools within .5 kilometers of methane-producing landfills. This information is crucial for targeting awareness and mitigation efforts in areas most affected by environmental hazards. I determined the wealthiest census tract with a landfill, shedding light on socioeconomic disparities related to landfill proximity. Lastly, I located the closest methane landfill to Topeka Charter School for Advanced Studies in Northridge, California, utilizing different search thresholds to ensure accuracy. This project is of utmost importance as it addresses environmental justice concerns, highlights socioeconomic disparities, and provides valuable insights for informed decision-making by city officials and stakeholders. It underscores the power of GIS technology in addressing complex environmental challenges and promoting a healthier, more equitable future for all residents of Los Angeles. It is just another day at the office for a GIS Superhero. Lets go on Another Mission! !
